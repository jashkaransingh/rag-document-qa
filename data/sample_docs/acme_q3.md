# Acme Robotics Quarterly Update

Acme Robotics finished Q3 with revenue of $42 million, up 38 percent year over year.
The growth was driven primarily by industrial automation contracts in the
automotive sector, where our autonomous welding cells were deployed at three
major OEM facilities.

## Key milestones

The Mark VII chassis began shipping to customers in July. It improved payload
capacity to 120 kilograms while reducing weight by 18 percent compared to the
Mark VI. Early customer feedback has been strongly positive, with deployment
times cut roughly in half.

Our software platform, AcmeOS 4.2, was released in September. The major change
was a switch from ROS 1 to ROS 2, which involved rewriting our motion planning
stack from scratch. We also added a low-latency vision pipeline that runs
inference at 90 frames per second on our standard hardware.

## Challenges

Supply chain pressure on rare-earth motors continues. We have been qualifying
two alternate suppliers in Vietnam and Mexico to reduce single-source risk.

The Mark VII shipment timeline slipped two weeks due to a firmware issue with
the new joint controllers. The bug was traced to a CAN bus timing assumption
that did not hold at higher gear reduction ratios.

## Outlook

We expect Q4 revenue between $48 and $52 million. Two large defense contracts
are in final negotiation that could lift this further if they close before
year end.
